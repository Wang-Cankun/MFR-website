We also provided R function script files for computing gene-pair features and MFR values, and a guidance file, an example R data file with the result file and a gene expression R data file for teaching and showing others how to use these resource and tools.

Resource

  MFR model was trained over the gene-pair samples collected from the COXPRESdb, KEGG and TRRUST databases and the dataset of the study of Pan at el., and, in which the positive gene pairs are the coexpressed gene pairs, gene pairs with PPIs, involved in same pathways, or with transcriptional regulatory relationships, and the negatives are the opposites. We have also used MFR model to measure relatedness of the gene pairs in the geneFriends, DIP, cancer and KEGG-metabolism datasets, in which the positive gene pairs in the geneFriends dataset are the gene pairs with co-expressions collected from the geneFriends database and the negatives are gene pairs by permutation of the first genes with the second genes in the positive gene pairs; the positive gene pairs in the DIP dataset are gene pairs with PPIs collected from the DIP database and the negatives are by permutation of the first genes with the second genes in the positive gene pairs; the gene pair in the TCGA cancer datasets are composited by the metabolic genes up-regulated in four cancer types including BLCA, BRCA, COAD and LUAD, and the gene pairs in KEGG-metabolism dataset are composited by the genes involved in metabolism pathways collected from the KEGG database. All the datasets and the results mentioned above are downloaded freely for further verification and biological analysis.
